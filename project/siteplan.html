Kevine Uwase Portfolio Site Plan

This document outlines the planning and design process for my personal portfolio website. The purpose of the site is to showcase my skills, projects, and professional experience in web development and design. It will feature a clean and modern design that reflects my personal brand, ensuring a professional presentation across all devices. The site plan includes the proposed site name, purpose, color scheme, typography, and wireframe layout, which together will guide the development of the final project.

Site Name

Kevine Uwase Portfolio

This name represents my personal brand and will serve as an online space to display my work and experience. It’s simple, memorable, and clearly communicates the site’s purpose.

Site Purpose

The goal of this portfolio website is to highlight my skills, completed projects, and professional achievements. It will provide an accessible and visually appealing way for potential employers or clients to learn about my abilities, background, and contact information.

Scenarios

  • What projects has Kevine completed recently?
  • How can I contact her for a collaboration or job opportunity?
  • What skills and technologies does she use in her work?

Color Scheme

  • Primary: #4A90E2 (Blue) – used for headings and highlights
  • Accent: #F5A623 (Orange) – used for buttons and hover effects
  • Background: #FFFFFF (White) – main background color
  • Text: #333333 (Dark Gray) – used for body text

Typography

  • Poppins: Used for all headings and titles.
  • Open Sans: Used for body text and paragraphs.

Testing and Validation

  • Validate HTML and CSS using W3C tools.
  • Check responsiveness across mobile, tablet, and desktop.
  • Ensure accessibility: proper contrast, alt text, and readable fonts.
  • Test all links and contact forms for proper functionality.

Submission

All files (siteplan.html and styles.css) will be uploaded to the course GitHub repository. The repository URL will then be submitted through Canvas for grading.
